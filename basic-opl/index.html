About E-Cell Karunya

We at E-Cell believe in making Karunya Institute of Technology and Sciences a leading startup campus by bringing an entrepreneurial culture among the students and faculty of the institute. We also believe that experience is the best teacher and hence, provide entrepreneurial education to students, who aspire to start their own venture as well as help them discover ideas that can be developed into products or services. E-Cell provides an entrepreneurial platform for turning dreams into reality by the means of mentorship, provision of office space and financial support. It enables the future entrepreneurs to mark their niche in the market and develop an unforgettable relationship with the world. However, the courses help the students to understand all the aspects of a startup that need to be taken care of to convert it into a successful venture. After completion of courses our well qualified and knowledgeable mentors will guide and provide them with all the assistance required for the growth of their venture at an exponential rate.

About NEN

NEN stands for National Entrepreneurship Network which is a Wadhwani Foundation initiative that believes in transforming valid ideas of students and professionals into ventures. Their goal is to create a National culture of entrepreneurship. It empowers millions of global students with entrepreneurial mindset and skills, by providing them with high quality experiential entrepreneurial education. They train the students by enhancing their job prospects, encouraging intrapreneurship and driving long-term entrepreneurship. Their main focus is to change the student’s perspective towards the problem. Furthermore, it gives students practical knowledge and also a chance to step into other’s shoes to experience entrepreneurship in real-time fashion.

About MHRD

The Ministry of Human Resource Development, formerly Ministry of Education (until 25 September 1985), is responsible for the development of human resources in India. The Ministry is held currently by Ramesh Pokhriyal and is divided into two departments: the Department of School Education and Literacy, which deals with primary, secondary and higher secondary education, adult education and literacy, and the Department of Higher Education, which deals with university education, technical education, scholarship etc. The erstwhile Ministry of Education now functions under these two departments, as of 26 September 1985.
